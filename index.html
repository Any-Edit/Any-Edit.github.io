ANYEDIT: A Unified Framework for Speech and Singing Voice Editing in Real-World Acoustic Environments

Yunjia Zhang1, Junan Zhang1, Jing Yang2, Xueyao Zhang1, Fan Fan2, Zhizheng Wu1
1 The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen
2 Central Media Technology Institute, Huawei

Abstract

We present ANYEDIT, a unified framework for speech and singing voice editing in real-world acoustic environments. Recent unified generative models already achieve strong speech and singing generation in clean conditions, but real-world vocal editing remains challenging because the model must revise content while simultaneously preserving speaker identity, prosody, and the surrounding acoustic scene. A straightforward solution is to directly model noisy edited audio end to end, yet this entangles semantic editing with environmental acoustics and can weaken the core vocal generation capability. We therefore adopt a two-stage design that preserves clean-domain editing ability while deferring acoustic rendering to a separate stage.

Methodology

Figure 1: Overall architecture of the ANYEDIT framework.

The AR model operates in a clean semantic space to predict prosody and content tokens, supported by a noise-robust teacher-student tokenizer. A flow-matching acoustic model then renders mel-spectrograms conditioned on source acoustic context.

ANYEDITBENCH

ANYEDITBENCH covers 12 subsets (3 splits × 2 modalities × 2 languages), totaling 600 evaluation items:

  • Clean: single-span and multi-span edits on clean recordings.
  • Simulated: diverse noise and reverberation settings.
  • Real-world: in-the-wild speech and singing recordings.
